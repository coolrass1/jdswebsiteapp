## How to use this checklist

Work through each section and tick off items as you complete them. Each item includes guidance on what content to add and where to put it on your website.

---

## 1. SRA Logo — Rule 4.1

- [x] **Add the SRA clickable logo to your website**  
  Download the official logo from the SRA website and place it in your footer (or header). It must link to `https://www.sra.org.uk/consumers/register/` so visitors can verify your firm is regulated.

---

## 2. Complaints Information — Rule 2.1

Create a dedicated **Complaints** page on your website and include all four of the following:

- [ ] **Your firm's own complaints procedure**  
  Explain how a client can complain to the firm — who to contact (name/role), how (email/letter), and how long you will take to respond (typically 8 weeks maximum).

- [ ] **SRA contact details**  
  Include the SRA as an escalation option if the client is unhappy with your response:  
  Solicitors Regulation Authority, The Cube, 199 Wharfside Street, Birmingham, B1 1RN  
  Tel: 0370 606 2555 | www.sra.org.uk

- [ ] **Legal Ombudsman (LeO) contact details**  
  PO Box 6806, Wolverhampton, WV1 9WJ  
  Tel: 0300 555 0333 | www.legalombudsman.org.uk

- [ ] **LeO time limits**  
  State that complaints to the Legal Ombudsman must normally be made within **one year** of the problem occurring, or within one year of when the client should reasonably have known about it.

## 3. Debt Recovery — Rule 1.5

**Location:** Add to or update `jdssolicitors.com/fees`

- [ ] **Cost information** — total, average, or range of fees  
  e.g. *"Our fees for debt recovery matters up to £100,000 typically range from £X to £X."*

- [ ] **Basis of costs** — hourly rate or fixed fee  
  State clearly whether you charge a fixed fee or an hourly rate, and what that rate is.  
  e.g. *"We charge a fixed fee of £X for letters before action, or an hourly rate of £X + VAT for contested matters."*

- [ ] **Disbursements** — likely additional costs  
  List expected disbursements and their typical costs, for example:  
  - Court issue fee (varies by claim value, set by HMCTS)  
  - Process server fees (approx. £X–£X)  
  - Enforcement fees (e.g. bailiff/High Court Enforcement Officer)

- [ ] **VAT information**  
  State clearly: *"All fees are subject to VAT at the current rate of 20%."* Also note which disbursements attract VAT.

- [ ] **What is included in the price**  
  List the specific services covered, for example:  
  - Letter before action  
  - Issuing County Court claim  
  - Obtaining judgment in default  
  - Enforcement proceedings

- [ ] **Key stages and timescales**  
  Provide a simple timeline, for example:  
  - Week 1: Letter before action sent  
  - Week 3–4: Issue County Court claim (if no response)  
  - Week 6–8: Obtain judgment in default  
  - Week 10+: Enforcement action if required
